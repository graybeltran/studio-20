The Great Divide

The Great Divide is a multimedia project that explores the extremes of education, labor, housing, and identity disparities through the personal narratives of eight New York City residents. In this project you will experience a series of opposing vignettes that showcase the extremes of New York City:
A recent New York City immigrant with limited skills who is struggling to support his family versus a well-educated immigrant who is living handsomely. A woman who struggles to find work because of her advanced age, versus a young woman enjoying flexibility in the type of work she does. A young student who wants to scrap a specialized high school test that prevented him from the school of his dreams, versus a student who says he works hard and wants to keep the test to weed out less competent students. And a drag queen who comes to term with her sexuality, versus a pastor who preaches against the evils of homosexuality.
The Great Divide was designed and produced by the graduate students in New York University’s Studio 20 program.
